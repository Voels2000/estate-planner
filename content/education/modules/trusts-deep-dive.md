## Trust Roles

A trust relationship generally includes:

- **Grantor/Settlor**: creates and funds the trust
- **Trustee**: manages trust assets
- **Beneficiary**: receives trust benefits under trust terms

## Revocable vs Irrevocable

- **Revocable trust**: usually changeable during life; often used for management continuity and probate efficiency
- **Irrevocable trust**: typically not freely changeable; often used for asset protection or transfer-tax planning objectives

## Funding Is the Critical Step

A trust only governs assets that are actually transferred into it or directed to it by beneficiary designations.

Unfunded trusts can leave intended outcomes unachieved.

## Common Educational Use Cases

- Revocable trust for continuity and probate planning
- ILIT concepts for life insurance ownership separation
- Charitable trust concepts for philanthropic + tax integration

> Educational note: trust drafting and implementation are legal services and should be handled by qualified counsel.
